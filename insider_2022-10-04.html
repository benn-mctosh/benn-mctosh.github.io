Past Tense Graduate Review of History

Past Tense provides a forum for developing scholars to gain experience with academic publishing. The journal publishes original research articles, book reviews and critical commentaries about issues in the field of history. It welcomes submissions from researchers of all disciplines and institutions on topics that concern a broad range of time periods and geographies.

Deadline: November 1, 2022 (for Summer 2023 Issue)

Three Minute Thesis®

Graduate Women in Science and the Graduate School Office of Professional Development

Can you explain your research in 3 minutes? Find out in the 2022-23 Three Minute Thesis® Competition! 3MT®is an international competition in which graduate students explain their research to a general audience. Use 3MT® to hone your communication skills; expand your research’s impact, and convey the significance of your work to funders, legislators, the media, and more. Students in research-based master’s and PhD programs from all disciplines can compete, and we especially encourage humanities students to join us. Finalists selected at semi-finals on November 2 will receive $100 cash prizes; finals will be held in February 2023 where winners receive up to $1,000 in cash prizes. 

Registration Deadline: October 19, 2022
Contact: Alexandra Fleagle | pa1@grad.wisc.edu

Asia in Motion: Memory, Preservation and Documentation

2023 AAS-in-Asia Conference, Daegu, Korea

The 2023 AAS-in-Asia conference seeks organized panels and roundtable session proposals on all aspects of Asian Studies. The conference will focus on the study of historical and cultural phenomena in Asia with respect to "memory," "preservation," and "documentation." Through these lenses, we intend to uncover the crisis inherent to the era of the fourth industrial revolution and discuss a future-oriented development of Asian studies while remembering and reflecting on the paths we have chosen for ourselves over the past centuries.

Deadline: October 31, 2022 at 5:00pm Eastern Time
Conference Dates: June 24–27, 2023
Contact: AASinAsia@asianstudies.org

Graduate Student Tracking System PAship

The Graduate School's Office of Academic Analysis, Planning and Assessment seeks a Project Assistant to support the Graduate Student Tracking System (GSTS). The GSTS is a system that allows students, advisors, and program staff, as well as the Graduate School, to view and track the academic progress towards degree requirements of all graduate students. We are looking for someone who has a detail-oriented brain that enjoys digging into academic program requirements and “translating” them into logical statements that can be configured in PeopleSoft (SIS).

20 hours per week, $24.82 per hour, partially remote

Deadline: October 7, 2022

Assistant Professor, Teaching Stream - Digital and Public History

University of Toronto Scarborough (UTSC)

UTSC invites applications for a full-time teaching stream position in the field of Digital and Public History. Applicants must be prepared to teach courses in digital and public history across all levels of undergraduate instruction, from large survey courses to research-intensive seminars. Ask A Historian


The Ask a Historian podcast is one way the Department of History gives meaning to the Wisconsin Idea—that education should transcend the walls of the classroom, and that the university’s work should benefit all those in the state and beyond. Every episode features an interview with a University of Wisconsin—Madison historian who answers a question about the past submitted by our listeners.

The generous support of the Department of History’s
Board of Visitors helps make Ask a Historian possible.
