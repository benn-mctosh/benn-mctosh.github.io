How do I use this?This sheet will let you to generate a random God-eater, or fill in the fields yourself.

Generating your character: Click here to randomly fill the entire sheet. You can click the 🎲 next to a field to re-roll just that option, or click the 🎲 🎲 at the top of a section to re-roll that entire section. You can also manually adjust any options you desire, and customize your Creed, Appearance, and Inventory.

Learning the rules: Wherever you see a [⚠️] you can click it for some introductions or reminders about the rules, but the rules are best learnt at Eat God's home, here.

Playing your character: You can use the browser version of this sheet for playing Eat God—it lets you update your inventory, stress, and obstinancy—but it won't save your work. Ideally, though, it’s printable—try printing with the minimum margins, and at an 80% zoom level.

Name

⚠️ Guard your True Name carefully.God-eaters customarily keep their true names secret, adopting formal names drawn from classics of philosophy, subtle poetic allusions, and the sayings of great heroes. These names range from a handful of words to a full paragraph in length, and are accompanied by a short form of no more than three syllables for everyday use.. The default assumption is that each God-eater in a particular group knows the true names of all other members of that group unless you explicitly decide otherwise. You need not decide what your God-eater's true name is or communicate it to the other players for their characters to know it. [more about names]

Facets 🎲🎲

⚠️ It’s all Greek to me?

Your God-eater's Facets are the core of your mastery and the means through which you assert your will upon the cosmos. Each Facet is both a way of being and a way of relating to the world around you – and, most importantly, when rolling a test, the most relevant Facet provides the target number. Use the dice to roll, or pick from one of these four standard arrays: 7/5/3, 7/4/4, 6/6/3, 6/5/4.

Ethos rules your acts of skill—it’s used to act with care and diligence, to anticipate danger and consequences, and to get people to do what you want by presenting yourself as trustworthy and reasonable.

Pathos rules your acts of passion—it’s used to act with force and spontaneity, to resist social pressure, and to get people to do what you want by inspiring, intimidating, and appealing to the baser urges.

Logos rules your acts of cunning—it’s used to act with strategy and guile, to pierce deceptions, and to get people to do what you want with clever arguments that confuse your foes, your allies, and occasionally yourself.

If your score in one of these is lower or higher than the norm (5) you may select an adjective to help you role-play a character with such a talented or flawed facet of their character. [more about facets]

Traits 🎲🎲

⚠️ What’re these?

Traits give you two benefits. First, you can do whatever the Trait says you can. Second, when you Make a Test that you can bring the Trait to bear on (that is, “Tag” that Trait), you get to roll an extra die—increasing the chance of both success and of cataclysm. The actions a Trait lets you do “with effort” are “Effortful”; you can only maintain one “Effortful” effect at once, whether from Traits, Rebellious Arts, or something else [more about Traits].

Some traits have additional options, flagged by language like “[roll or choose...]” in the description and the appearance of an additional dropdown menu.

Rebellious Art 🎲🎲

⚠️ Remember, sustaining any Art is Effortful

What sets you apart from your peers and elevates you as a God-killing hero is not the strength of your arm or the keenness of your eye, but your initiation into the Rebellious Arts: esoteric discplines which allow you to see through a small part of the lie that God tells to the world.

Employing the Rebellious Arts is effortful; i.e., you may not activate an Art while sustaining another effortful effect, nor may you use another effortful effect while sustaining an Art. In addition, you must spend one point of Obstinacy each time an Art is activated.

The Arts don't require tests; however, if you use an Art in conjunction with a test, you may roll one extra die. This works like spending a point of Obstinacy to add a die to the test, and is subject to all the usual restrictions thereof. If an Art's effect is sustained, you may claim this benefit on any one test made under its auspices for each activation [more about Rebellious Arts].
