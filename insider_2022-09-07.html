History Grad Insider

Sept 7th, 2022

Welcome back to school, and back to the History Grad Insider, the History department's fortnightly list of jobs and other opportunities for grad students like us. Our aim is to put all such adverts in one place, rather than cluttering your inbox throughout the week.

This newsletter is special: it has all the updates that have been building up in Lisa's inbox all summer, including seven tenure-track jobs — though we regularly get advertisements for those, so read to the end of all our newsletters!

Have a good start to the school year!

HistoriFans special series on 1990s pop culture

HistoriFans is looking for contributors who want to geek out and write articles about the connections between contemporary pop culture and their scholarly interests. We are interested in articles that connect pedagogy, theory, sociology, history, anthropology, and/or other areas of inquiry to major fandoms. We also welcome digital humanities articles/projects. Every article that we receive will go through a full review process. 

Currently accepting pitches both for general articles and a special series on 1990s pop culture. Writing should be ~1500 words, and aimed at a popular audience. Appears to be uncompensated.

Deadline: Pitches seem to be accepted on a rolling basis
Contact: Danielle Sanchez | dsanchez@coloradocollege.edu

Conferences

"From the Archive Looking Out: Pushing History Beyond Its Comfort Zone"

[Sixth annual Stony Brook History Graduate Student Assoc. Conference]


From Kelly Lytle Hernández’s “Rebel Archive '' to interdisciplinary subfields like animal-human history, scholars now interrogate the power invested in traditional archives and look beyond to reveal richer historical narratives. This year’s conference provides a platform to scholars who challenge disciplinary norms by using unexplored sources and methodologies, focusing on new perspectives and topics, and exploring uses of historical research beyond academia.

Conference dates: September 29-30, 2022

Robert H. N. Ho Family Foundation Program in Buddhist Studies Fellowships & Grants

The American Council of Learned Societies (ACLS) invites applications for The Robert H. N. Ho Family Foundation Program in Buddhist Studies 2022-23 competitions. For work that will expand the understanding and interpretation of Buddhist thought in scholarship and society, strengthen international networks of Buddhist scholars, and increase the visibility of new knowledge and research on Buddhist traditions.
Dissertation Fellowships: One-year stipends to PhD candidates for full-time preparation of dissertations. $30,000 for 10 months.
Early Career Research Fellowships: One-year stipends to pre-tenure scholars for research and writing. Up to $70,000 for 12 months.
Translation Grants in Buddhist Studies: One-year awards supporting collaborative or individual projects for translations of important texts in order to make them available to communities of scholarship and Buddhist practice. Up to $50,000 for 12 months

All 3 Deadlines: November 16, 2022, 9 pm EST
Contact: BuddhistStudies@acls.org

Job Postings

Assistant Professor in Eurasian History

Bucknell University

The Department of History at Bucknell University invites applications for a full-time, tenure-track position in Eurasia with a focus on Russian imperial relations, at the level of assistant professor, to begin in August 2023. Research approach and geographical area are open, with particular interest taken in candidates focusing on gender and/or sexuality. The successful candidate will teach introductory courses on the Russian Empire and/or the Soviet Union or post-Soviet Russia, and upper-level courses in the candidate's areas of expertise.

Deadline: October 9, 2022 (application review begins Sept. 12)
Contact: Jennifer Thomson | jct021@bucknell.edu
H-net post | Bucknell History Dept.

Harvard-Newcomen Postdoctoral Fellowship in Business History

Harvard Business School

Twelve months’ residence, study, and research at Harvard Business School, commencing July 1, 2023. The fellow will have the opportunity to contribute to course development in MBA and doctoral courses in business history. They will also take part in a weekly research seminar in the fall of each year in which leading scholars from around the world present their work.

Deadline: November 1, 2022
Contact: David J. Shorten | dshorten@hbs.edu

US Naval Academy (3 postings)

US Race & Ethnicity | Modern Russia & E. Europe | Ancient Mediterranean

All positions: Employment will begin as early as the fall semester of 2023 (late July 2023). US Citizenship Required.

Tenure-track Assistant or Associate Professor specializing in the history of race and ethnicity in the United States: Preference is at the Assistant Professor level, but candidates with appropriate experience will be considered at the level of Associate Professor without tenure. Candidates who focus on the twentieth-century, especially civil rights issues, are preferred. The successful applicant will be expected to teach a survey course in either modern world history or, if they prefer, a survey of American naval history as part of the institution’s core curriculum. They will also offer upper level courses in areas of specialization, as well as majors-only seminars.

Tenure-track Assistant Professor specializing in the modern history of Russia and Eastern Europe: Candidates who focus on the history of this region in twentieth-century, especially the Soviet era, are preferred. The successful applicant will be expected to teach a survey course in either modern world history or, if they prefer, a survey of American naval history as part of the institution’s core curriculum. They will also offer upper-level courses in areas of specialization, as well as majors-only seminars.

Tenure-track Assistant Professor specializing in the history of the ancient Mediterranean: Candidates who focus on the history of ancient Greece and/or Rome are preferred. The successful applicant will be expected to teach a survey course in ancient history as part of the institution’s core curriculum. They will also offer upper level courses in areas of specialization, as well as majors-only seminars.

Review of applications begins: October 3, 2022

Assistant Professor focusing on Globalised Modern Science, Technology, and Medicine

University of King's College (Canada)

The History of Science and Technology Program (HOST) and the Foundation Year Program (FYP) at the University of King’s College invite applications for a tenure-track position at the rank of Assistant Professor, commencing July 1, 2023. In addition to specialized expertise in globalised modern science, candidates should have a broad competency and interest in liberal arts teaching.

Ask A Historian


The Ask a Historian podcast is one way the Department of History gives meaning to the Wisconsin Idea—that education should transcend the walls of the classroom, and that the university’s work should benefit all those in the state and beyond. Every episode features an interview with a University of Wisconsin—Madison historian who answers a question about the past submitted by our listeners.

The generous support of the Department of History’s
Board of Visitors helps make Ask a Historian possible.
