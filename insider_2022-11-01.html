Anna Julia Cooper Workshop in Black History

University of Maryland Dept. of History

The Anna Julia Cooper Workshop in Black History (The Cooper Workshop) features scholars from various disciplines researching and writing on Black history in the United States and the world. Cooper was the first African American woman to earn a PhD in History. As a works-in-progress series, we discuss pre-circulated, unpublished papers. The Cooper Workshop draws an interdisciplinary community from the D.C., Maryland, and Virginia area with expertise in a wide reach of the field. We host six sessions per academic year. Papers will be circulated seven days in advance of the workshop.

For the 2022-23 academic year, the workshop will meet via Zoom on Fridays, 4:00-5:30 p.m., ET.

Talk and Research Design Symposium with John A. Marston

Presented by the Theravada Studies Symposium

John A. Marston, Professor-Researcher at the Center for Asian and African Studies, El Colegio de México, will join the Theravada Studies Symposium this Friday for two events, the first a talk on his research and the second a discussion on research design.

I. “Dharmawara Mahathera: More Thoughts about a Transnational Cambodian Monk”

November 4, 2022 @ 12:00 pm - 1:30 pm 

206 Ingraham Hall

Dharmawara Mahathera (1889-1999) was a colorful Cambodian monk resident in India from the mid-1930s until the mid-1970s and the United States from then until his death. This talk, while summarizing some of my past work on his influence on the international stance of newly-independent Cambodia while in India, will shift the focus to other aspects of his career as a monk, healer, and teacher: his time in Siam and Burma before going to India, the life at his temple near Delhi, and the contacts with young Westerners which led him to go to Great Britain and the U.S.

No RSVP required, but contact seasia@seasia.wisc.edu for accommodations or dietary restrictions.

II. Roundtable Discussion — "Research Design: Interview and Oral History Method"

November 4, 2022 @ 4:30 pm - 6:00 pm

Room 5243 (Curti Lounge), Mosse Humanities Building

This event will be a laid-back, informal roundtable discussion on designing research utilizing the interview and oral history method. The conversation will be led by Dr.John A. Marston as well as by other scholars/members associated with Theravada Studies Group UW-Madison.
