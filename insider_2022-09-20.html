Calls for Papers

"Between Conflict and Connection"

13th Annual Texas A&M Grad/Undergrad History Student Conference

This conference seeks scholarly discussion on the significance of historical interactions, both peaceful and violent. In selecting this theme, the conference focuses on histories of struggle, compromise, and identity. The conference welcomes papers on both violent and nonviolent conflict and struggle, and everything in between. Undergraduate and graduate students are both encouraged to apply.

Abstract Deadline: November 18, 2022

Conference: February 17–18, 2023
Contact: tamuconference2023@gmail.com

Allan Nevins Dissertation Prize (US History)

Society of American Historians (SAH)

Awarded annually for the best-written doctoral dissertation on a significant subject in American history. The prizewinning work is published by one of the distinguished houses that support the prize. The SAH defines history broadly and welcomes manuscripts on American arts, literature, and science, as well as biographical studies of Americans in any walk of life. The dissertation must have been successfully defended between January 1, 2022 and December 31, 2022. $2,000 Prize.

Submission: The History Department can send up to two submissions to the SAH. If you are interested, send your submission to Leslie Abadie, Graduate Program Manager, at abadie@wisc.edu by December 15, 2022
SAH Contact: amhistsociety@columbia.edu

Business History Doctoral Colloquium

Hosted by the Business History Conference (BHC)

Typically limited to ten students, the colloquium is open to doctoral candidates who are pursuing dissertation research within the broad field of business history. Most participants are in year 3 or 4 or their degree program, though some students participate at later stages after their research evolves. Participants work intensively with a distinguished group of BHC-affiliated scholars, discussing dissertation proposals, relevant literatures and research strategies, and career trajectories.
Colloquium participants have a choice of pre-circulating either a 15-page dissertation prospectus or updated research plan; or a draft dissertation chapter.

Deadline: December 9, 2022
Colloquium Dates: March 7–8, 2022
Contact: Eric Godelier | eric.godelier@polytechnique.edu

Assistant or early Associate Professor, Trans-Asia Critical Humanities

Cornell University Dept. of Asian Studies

This professorship in Trans-Asia Critical Humanities reexamines Asian conceptually within and beyond area studies. Applications are welcome from candidates working in, or across, any region(s) of Asia. Candidates must posses proficiency in one or more Asian languages as well as the demonstrated ability to speak conceptually to broad audiences across disciplinary and area studies boundaries. Fields of potential, but not exhaustive, interest to the department include: critical race theory; displacement; new materialism; critical area studies; migration; and intermediality.

Deadline: October 14, 2022
Contact: Kim Scott | asianstudiesjobs@cornell.edu

Ask A Historian


The Ask a Historian podcast is one way the Department of History gives meaning to the Wisconsin Idea—that education should transcend the walls of the classroom, and that the university’s work should benefit all those in the state and beyond. Every episode features an interview with a University of Wisconsin—Madison historian who answers a question about the past submitted by our listeners.
